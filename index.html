Past

In my adult working career, it has always been important to me that I have worked in organisations that have a positive impact on society...

I have always wanted to use my skills to help others. With that in mind, after I graduated university with a degree in IT for Business, I worked in the public sector, specifically the NHS, in various roles understanding how to work with people and how complex organisations work.

This inspired me to learn counselling skills via part-time courses for 2 years, while working full time. This helped develop my empathy and listening skills which have helped me in my personal and professional life to this current day.

In around 2015, I started to get interested in web development and the ways in which apps/tools could be built to allow efficiency in businesses. I spent some time doing a few introduction courses inlcuding codeacademy.

In 2016, I became a student of The Firehose Project, an online 16-week full-stack web development bootcamp course. I learnt how to build apps using HTML/CSS and Ruby on Rails, and how to work in a team of developers.

To learn more about what I learnt on the course, please find links to my study blog and my published medium article, here or above in the navigation.

I really enjoyed this experience and want to continue my coding journey to become a web developer.
